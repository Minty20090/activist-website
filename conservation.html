What is conservation?

Conservation is primarily defined as the act of protecting Earth's natural resources and ecosystems for future generations.

There are many different forms of conservation!

Habitat Conservation

Habitat conservation seeks to protect or restore natural habitats. Habitat conservation can happen in many ways, such as prohibiting human activity in certain areas or reintroducing native species to an area that has been impacted by human activity.

Species Conservation

Species conservation seeks to protect or restore animal species. Species conservation is a way to bring back species from the brink of extinction. Reintroduction programs or captive breeding programs give animals a head-start in life, giving them an advantage when they're released back into their natural habitats, which can be key for their long-term survival.
